Student Showcase

Spaces built by students in the Level 2 course. Every one runs on free CPU.

These Spaces were built during Sessions 10-11 of the course. Each student chose a model from their Collection, designed an interface for a specific audience, and deployed it on Hugging Face's free CPU tier. The Spaces here represent the best of each cohort — nominated by peers and refined through iteration.

What Makes a Showcase Space

To be featured here, a student Space should demonstrate at least three things from the course:

  • A clear audience — designed for a specific person, not "everyone" (Chapter 8)
  • A CPU strategy — uses one of the patterns from Chapter 3: small model, API wrapper, or no inference
  • At least one research method applied — tested with adversarial testing, fairness auditing, parameter sweep, or another method

Future Cohorts

This page grows with each cohort. If you're a student in the course and want your Space featured here, focus on the craft checklist in Chapter 8. The Spaces that make the showcase are the ones where someone clearly thought about who it's for, what it should do, and how it should handle the unexpected.

For instructors: After Demo Day (Session 12), nominate 3-5 Spaces from the cohort to feature here. Include the Space URL, a one-sentence description, which CPU pattern they used, and which research methods they applied. The showcase serves as both recognition for current students and inspiration for future ones.
